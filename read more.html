Udupi is a coastal district in the Indian state of Karnataka, known for its beautiful beaches, temples, and educational institutions. The name "Udupi" is believed to have come from the Sanskrit words "Udu" and "Pa," meaning "stars" and "Lord," respectively. The city is a great center of Sanskrit learning, imparted through the Ashta Mathas (Eight Maths): Puttige, Pejavara, Palimaru, Adamaru, Shirur, Sodhe, Krishnapura,cc and Kaniyoor. dupi is also famous for its vegetarian food, served in the South Indian style, which can be found in Udupi restaurants all over India. The city is at its best during festivals like Holi, Janmashtami, and the biennial Paryayothsava, where special ceremonies, religious discourses, rathotsava, and temple elephant processions take place.
